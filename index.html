Usability/User Experience study at CMU

Project Type: Research Project

Project Date: Spring 2018

Project Supervisor: Professor Lorrie Faith Cranor

See Research Poster

Privacy issues have become a serious problem in today's world. Redmorph is a powerful app that helps improve user privacy when surfing the internet. At Carnegie Mellon, we conducted a study to assess the usability of this handful application. The result is a poster which was accepted by and presented at the 14th Symposium on Usable Privacy and Security (SOUPS 2018) at Baltimore.

Sharing Experience in Virtual Reality

Project Type: Research Project

Project Date: Spring 2017

Project Supervisor: Professor Liwei Chan

I am captivated by virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) technologies because of their huge potential in diverse fields and industries; from entertainment to education to high-end medical equipment, these technologies will shape people’s lives in the future. In this project, I explored interaction between a head-mounted device (HMD) user and other users without HMDs. A particular focus is the transition between different interaction phases.
